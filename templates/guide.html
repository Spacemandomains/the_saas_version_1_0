Quick Start

1
Create your account
Sign up with your email and password. Enter your first name and company name — these personalize your AI C-Suite experience.
2
Create two Google Docs
Source Doc — This is where you write your "Dear CPO" messages. The AI CPO reads from this doc.
Output Doc — This is where the AI CPO writes its Daily Recap and CPO Brief. We recommend keeping these separate so your notes stay clean.
Recap Doc — (Optional) A customer-facing document where a polished daily CPO Recap is written every day at your chosen time (default 6 PM).
3
Open the AI CPO from the C-Suite page
Click the AI CPO card on your AI C-Suite page to open the CPO Dashboard.
4
Configure CPO Settings
Scroll to the CPO Settings section on the dashboard:
  • Paste your Source Google Doc URL or document ID
  • Paste your Output Google Doc URL or document ID
  • Select your timezone

Write "Dear CPO" in your source doc followed by your message. The AI CPO will detect it automatically and generate output. No special headings needed.
That's it! Once active, the AI CPO monitors your source doc on a schedule you control. No manual triggers needed — just write and the AI handles the rest.

Setting Up Your Google Docs

You need two Google Docs (or one, but two is recommended):

Source Doc (Your Notes)

  • Create a new Google Doc or use an existing one
  • This is where you write your "Dear CPO" messages — thoughts, decisions, questions
  • The AI CPO reads from this doc but never overwrites your entries
  • The only thing the CPO adds here is its daily question for you

Output Doc (CPO Writes Here)

  • Create a separate Google Doc for AI CPO output
  • The Daily Recap and CPO Brief get written here under dated headings
  • Keeps your source doc clean and your CPO output organized
To get your document ID: open the Google Doc, look at the URL — the ID is the long string between /d/ and /edit. You can also paste the full URL and the system will extract the ID for you.

How the AI CPO Works

The AI CPO runs on autopilot. Here's the cycle:

  1. Monitors — Checks your source Google Doc on a schedule (default: every 30 minutes)
  2. Detects "Dear CPO" messages — Uses a content fingerprint to detect when you've written new messages
  3. Generates output — Produces a Daily Recap and CPO Brief using AI
  4. Writes to output doc — Appends the output under dated headings in your output Google Doc
  5. Asks you a question — Appends one strategic question back to your source doc for you to answer next time
  6. Daily CPO Recap — At your chosen time (default 6 PM), writes a polished, customer-facing product update to your Recap Doc
The monitoring interval is configurable from 5 minutes to 24 hours. You can change it anytime in the CPO Settings on the dashboard.

Google Doc Format

For best results, structure your source Google Doc like this:

Dear CPO,

Had a call with two potential customers today.
They both asked about team collaboration features.
Decided to push back the mobile app to Q3.
Need to hire a frontend developer soon.
Revenue hit $4k MRR this week.

Dear CPO,

Shipped the onboarding flow redesign.
Churn rate dropped from 8% to 6% this month.
What should I focus on this week?

Key formatting tips:

  • Start each message with "Dear CPO" — that's how the AI knows you're talking to it
  • Write naturally — bullet points, paragraphs, questions all work
  • Include decisions, metrics, conversations, blockers, and ideas
  • Ask questions directly and the CPO will address them
  • Write as many "Dear CPO" messages as you want — they'll all be picked up

Assigning Tasks

You can assign tasks to the CPO by including "task:" in your message:

Dear CPO,
task: Build the new onboarding flow by March 15
task: Set up customer feedback survey
Also, we had a great call with Acme Corp today.
  • "task:" or "assign:" — creates a new task
  • "by [date]" or "due [date]" — sets a deadline (e.g., "by Friday", "by March 15", "due 2026-03-01")
  • "done:", "completed:", or "finished:" — marks a matching task as complete (e.g., "done: onboarding flow")

Tasks show up on your CPO Dashboard and the CPO will reference them in the Daily Brief — reminding you about deadlines and flagging overdue items.

What the AI CPO Writes

Every time new "Dear CPO" messages are detected, the AI CPO generates two sections in your output doc:

Daily Recap

A structured summary of what happened:

  • Outcome of the day — One sentence: what moved forward and toward what goal
  • What happened — 3-7 items with context and why each matters
  • Decision made — The most important decision today. If none, the CPO proposes one
  • Non-core topics — Anything you mentioned outside the main priority, separated out
  • Blockers & risks — Issues that could slow you down

Daily CPO Brief

Actionable executive guidance:

  • Focus (next 14 days) — Your single priority with reasoning
  • Next action — ONE specific, executable thing to do next (not a list — one clear move)
  • One metric to watch — The number that matters most right now and why
  • Kill list — Max 3 things to stop doing today, with reasons
  • Non-core topics — Acknowledged but separated from strategic guidance
  • One question for founder — A strategic question appended to your source doc

Settings Guide

Shared Settings (Settings page)

These apply across your entire AI C-Suite:

  • Profile — Your name, company name, and company logo
  • Team — Invite co-founders and team members by email

CPO Settings (on the CPO Dashboard)

These are specific to the AI CPO agent:

  • Source Google Doc — The doc the CPO reads your "Dear CPO" messages from
  • Output Google Doc — The doc the CPO writes recaps and briefs to
  • CPO Recap Doc — A customer-facing doc for daily product updates (optional)
  • Recap Time — When the daily customer recap is generated (default 6:00 PM)
  • Timezone — Controls timestamps in the CPO output
  • AI CPO Active toggle — Turn monitoring on or off
  • Monitoring interval — How often the CPO checks for new messages (5 min to 24 hours)
Each AI agent in the C-Suite will have its own settings area on its dashboard. The shared Settings page is for profile and team info that applies to all agents.

Adding Your Team

You can invite co-founders, advisors, and team members:

  1. Go to Settings from the sidebar
  2. Scroll to the Team section
  3. Enter their email and select their role (Co-Founder, Advisor, or Team Member)
  4. Click Send Invite

Team members will be able to sign up and join your company's AI C-Suite.

FAQ

How often does the AI CPO check my doc?

By default, every 30 minutes. You can change this to anywhere between 5 minutes and 24 hours in the CPO Settings on the dashboard.

Does the AI CPO edit my source doc?

No. Your source doc is read-only to the CPO. The only thing it adds is one question for you at the end of each run. All output goes to the separate output doc.

What if I don't set up an output doc?

If no output doc is configured, the CPO falls back to writing in the source doc. But we strongly recommend using a separate output doc to keep things clean.

Will the CPO run if there are no new "Dear CPO" messages?

No. It uses a content fingerprint to detect changes. If no new "Dear CPO" messages are found since the last check, it skips that cycle.

What are the other AI agents?

The AI C-Suite will include a CTO, CFO, COO, CMO, and CHRO — all coming soon. Each will have its own dashboard and settings, and they'll share the same output docs to keep everything in one place.

Can I pause the AI CPO?

Yes. Toggle the AI CPO Active switch off either on the dashboard status card or in the CPO Settings section. The CPO stops monitoring immediately.
